Intel Sustainability Timeline

Explore the key milestones in Intel’s journey toward a more sustainable future. Discover how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet!

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore establish Intel in Silicon Valley, setting the stage for decades of innovation in microprocessors and technology.

1968

Intel is Founded

Intel introduces the world’s first commercial microprocessor, the 4004, revolutionizing computing and enabling the development of personal computers.

1971

First Microprocessor (4004) Launches

Intel becomes one of the first tech companies to start voluntarily reporting its environmental impact, laying the groundwork for future sustainability initiatives.

1994

Begins Voluntary Environmental Reporting

Intel becomes the largest voluntary corporate purchaser of green power in the U.S. under the EPA’s Green Power Partner Program, significantly reducing its carbon footprint.

2008

Largest Corporate Purchaser of Green Power

Intel opens its first LEED-certified building, a design center in Haifa, Israel, demonstrating its commitment to sustainable architecture and energy efficiency.

2010

First LEED-Certified Building

Intel reduces its absolute greenhouse gas emissions by more than 60% below 2007 levels, surpassing its 20% reduction goal ahead of schedule.

2012

Major Greenhouse Gas Reduction

Intel signs the U.S. White House Climate Pledge, committing to ongoing green power purchases and increased use of on-site alternative energy sources.

2015

White House Climate Pledge

By 2023, Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity globally, a 43% reduction in greenhouse gas emissions from its 2019 baseline, and net positive water use in four countries, including the U.S. and India.

2023

99% Renewable Electricity and Net Positive Water

Intel sets ambitious goals for 2030 and beyond: achieving 100% renewable electricity, net-zero greenhouse gas emissions in operations by 2040, reducing product carbon footprints, and increasing product energy efficiency tenfold.

RISE Strategy

Under its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy, Intel sets ambitious 2030 goals, including driving industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

Commitment

In 2022, Intel pledged to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This commitment builds on decades of environmental initiatives and partnerships across the semiconductor industry.

Water & Waste

Intel conserves billions of gallons of water annually and collaborates with local communities to restore watersheds. Intel also upcycles and recycles materials to reduce waste and advance a circular economy.

RISE Strategy
Intel’s RISE strategy stands for Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling. Through RISE, Intel commits to ethical business practices, diversity and inclusion, environmental sustainability, and using technology to solve global challenges. By 2030, Intel aims to collaborate with 30 countries and 30,000 institutions to empower more than 30 million people. The RISE strategy guides Intel’s efforts in climate action, water stewardship, waste reduction, and responsible sourcing, all while fostering innovation and positive societal impact.
Commitment
In 2022, Intel pledged to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions across its global operations (Scope 1 and 2) by 2040. This ambitious goal builds on decades of environmental initiatives, including investing $300 million in energy conservation, transitioning to 100% renewable electricity by 2030, and constructing new facilities to LEED standards. Intel is also working with suppliers and partners to address emissions throughout its value chain, aiming for a more sustainable technology ecosystem.
Water & Waste
Intel is a leader in corporate water stewardship, aiming to achieve net positive water use by 2030—restoring more water than it consumes globally. The company invests in water conservation and restoration projects with local communities, saving billions of gallons annually. Intel also prioritizes waste reduction by upcycling and recycling materials, supporting a circular economy, and collaborating with partners to advance sustainable manufacturing and resource management.
